🏗️ Pricing Architecture — Key Decisions
1
Bundle pricing, not add-on itemisation

$1/mo for Audience DNA alone isn't compelling. Research from SaaS pricing studies shows bundle pricing converts 3–4× better than itemised feature lists. Users think "value" not "cost" when they see one clear price. Heat at $22/mo is a clear, justified upgrade story.

2
First 2 blogs free → $15/10 block gateway

Brett confirmed: "Two feels better — the second email a month later and they've gotten two." At $0.08/post cost, free trial is $0.16 total. The $15/10 block at 94.7% margin is the first paid conversion anchor.

3
Cadence tiers locked behind bundles, not standalone

Bi-weekly requires Warm+ bundle. Weekly requires Heat+. This prevents cherry-picking the cadence upgrade alone at $9 while making bundles feel like natural progressions. Users see the full value picture before upgrading.

4
Brett's UX rule: single row, expands on demand

"Have an add configuration button and have it expand or pop up — we don't want a big commercial in front of somebody every time they go." The right panel shows one compact row. Users who aren't interested see nothing more, forever.

5
Growth plan is the 7-domain sweet spot

$59/mo ÷ 7 domains = $8.43/domain. Compare to Semrush Pro at $27.99/domain. SiteWarming is 3.3× cheaper and includes content generation Semrush doesn't offer. Lead all growth marketing with "from $5.90/domain."
